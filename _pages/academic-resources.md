## Recommended Reading

Some review articles that are friendly to new graduate students

* **A review on High-Energy Nuclear Phyiscs**   
Hannah Elfner and Berndt Müller, *The exploration of hot and dense nuclear matter: introduction to relativistic heavy-ion physics*, J. Phys. G 50 (2023) 10, 103001 ([link](https://inspirehep.net/literature/2168969))
* **A review on Jet Phyiscs in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions**    
Xin-Nian Wang and Urs Achim Wiedemann, *QGP@50: More than Four Decades of Jet Quenching*, arXiv:2508.18794 ([link](https://inspirehep.net/literature/2964047))


## Some useful tools
* **Pythia**: a Monte-Carlo event generator for proton-proton collisions ([link](https://pythia.org))
* **JETSCAPE**: a Monte-Carlo framework for high-energy nuclear collisions ([link](https://jetscape.org))
* **FONLL**: a generator for heavy quark spectra produced by proton-proton collisions ([link](http://www.lpthe.jussieu.fr/~cacciari/fonll/fonllform.html))
* **LBT**: a Monte-Carlo event generator we develop for jet interaction with the QGP (still under development) ([link](https://github.com/dyczzz/A-Color-Tracking-LBT-Model/))
